A beginner workout program should start with full body training to build a strong foundation and improve overall fitness. Full body workouts are recommended for the first 2 to 3 weeks because they help the body adapt to exercise and reduce the risk of injury.

During this phase, it is recommended to train 3 times per week with rest days in between. Each workout should include exercises that target all major muscle groups.

Example full body workout:
- Squats (legs): 3 sets of 10–12 reps
- Push-ups (chest): 3 sets of 8–12 reps
- Rows (back): 3 sets of 10–12 reps
- Shoulder press (shoulders): 3 sets of 10 reps
- Plank (core): 3 sets of 20–30 seconds

After 2 to 3 weeks, once the body adapts, it is recommended to switch to a split program, where different muscle groups are trained on different days.

3-day split example:
- Day 1: Chest and triceps
- Day 2: Back and biceps
- Day 3: Legs and shoulders

4-day split example:
- Day 1: Chest
- Day 2: Back
- Day 3: Legs
- Day 4: Shoulders and arms

5-day split example:
- Day 1: Chest
- Day 2: Back
- Day 3: Legs
- Day 4: Shoulders
- Day 5: Arms

Each workout should last about 45 to 60 minutes. Rest between sets should be about 60 to 90 seconds.

Cardio can be added after strength training or on separate days. Beginners can start with 10 to 20 minutes of light to moderate cardio, such as walking, cycling, or jogging. Cardio helps improve heart health and burn calories.

Stretching is important after every workout. It helps improve flexibility, reduce muscle soreness, and prevent injuries. Focus on stretching the muscles that were used during the workout.

Rest and recovery are also very important. Muscles grow and repair during rest, so it is recommended to have at least 1 to 2 rest days per week and get enough sleep.

Consistency is the most important factor. Following a simple and regular workout plan will lead to better results over time.
