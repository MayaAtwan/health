Cardio training and strength training are two main types of exercise, and both are important for overall health and fitness.

Cardio training, also known as aerobic exercise, focuses on improving heart and lung function. It involves continuous movement over a period of time. Examples include walking, running, cycling, swimming, and jumping rope.

The main benefits of cardio training include:
- Improved heart health
- Increased endurance
- Burning calories and supporting weight loss
- Better overall fitness

Strength training, also known as resistance training, focuses on building muscle and increasing strength. It involves exercises that use weights, resistance bands, or body weight.

The main benefits of strength training include:
- Building and maintaining muscle mass
- Increasing strength and power
- Improving metabolism
- Supporting bone health

Both types of training are important and should be combined for the best results.

For beginners, it is recommended to include:
- Strength training 2 to 4 times per week
- Cardio 2 to 5 times per week

Cardio can be done after strength training or on separate days. A balanced program that includes both cardio and strength training leads to better long-term health and performance.
