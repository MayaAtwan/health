Protein is an essential nutrient that plays a key role in building and repairing muscles, tissues, and cells in the body. It is also important for producing enzymes, hormones, and other body chemicals. Protein is especially important for people who exercise because it supports muscle recovery and helps muscles grow stronger after training.

There are two main types of protein: animal protein and plant-based protein.

Animal protein comes from foods such as chicken, beef, fish, eggs, and dairy products like milk, yogurt, and cheese. These sources are considered complete proteins because they contain all the essential amino acids that the body needs.

Plant-based protein comes from foods such as beans, lentils, chickpeas, tofu, nuts, and seeds. These are good options for people who follow a vegetarian or vegan diet. Some plant proteins may not contain all essential amino acids, so it is recommended to eat a variety of plant-based foods to get complete nutrition.

Common foods that contain protein include:
- Chicken breast (100g): about 30g protein
- Eggs (1 large egg): about 6g protein
- Greek yogurt (1 cup): about 10g protein
- Lentils (1 cup cooked): about 18g protein
- Chickpeas (1 cup cooked): about 15g protein
- Almonds (28g): about 6g protein
- Tuna (100g): about 25g protein
- Milk (1 cup): about 8g protein

Some popular protein products available in Israel include:
- Pro & Go protein yogurt drink: about 20g protein per bottle
- Muller protein yogurt: about 15–20g protein per serving
- Protein-enriched milk drinks: about 15–25g protein per bottle
- High-protein yogurts and puddings: about 15–25g protein per serving

The amount of protein a person needs depends on their age, weight, and activity level.

For people who do not exercise, the recommended amount is about 0.8 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight.

For people who exercise regularly or want to build muscle, the recommended amount is about 1.2 to 2.0 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight.

For example, a person who weighs 70 kg may need about 56 grams of protein per day if they are not active, and between 84 and 140 grams if they are physically active.

Eating enough protein helps improve muscle strength, supports recovery after workouts, helps maintain a healthy metabolism, and keeps the body functioning properly.
