Muscle gain, also known as muscle hypertrophy, happens when the body builds new muscle tissue in response to resistance training and proper nutrition.

To build muscle effectively, three main factors are required: strength training, adequate protein intake, and proper recovery.

Strength training should focus on progressive overload. This means gradually increasing the weight, repetitions, or intensity of exercises over time. Common exercises include squats, bench press, deadlifts, and rows.

It is recommended to train each muscle group 2 times per week for optimal results. Workouts should include 3 to 4 sets of 8 to 12 repetitions per exercise.

Protein is essential for muscle growth. It provides the building blocks (amino acids) needed to repair and grow muscle fibers. Good protein sources include chicken, fish, eggs, dairy products, legumes, and protein supplements if needed.

For muscle gain, it is recommended to consume about 1.2 to 2.0 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight.

In addition to protein, total calorie intake is important. To gain muscle, the body usually needs a small calorie surplus, meaning consuming slightly more calories than it burns.

Carbohydrates are also important because they provide energy for workouts. Healthy sources include rice, potatoes, oats, and fruits.

Recovery is a key part of muscle growth. Muscles grow during rest, not during the workout itself. It is important to:
- Sleep 7 to 9 hours per night
- Take rest days
- Avoid overtraining

Cardio can still be included, but it should not be excessive, as too much cardio can reduce muscle gain.

Consistency over time is the most important factor. Building muscle takes time, patience, and regular training.
