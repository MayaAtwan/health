Weight loss happens when the body burns more calories than it consumes. This is called a calorie deficit. A healthy and sustainable weight loss plan focuses on balanced nutrition, regular exercise, and good lifestyle habits.

A calorie deficit can be achieved by eating slightly less food, increasing physical activity, or combining both. It is important not to reduce calories too much, as this can harm the body and slow down metabolism.

A healthy diet for weight loss should include:
- Lean protein such as chicken, fish, eggs, and legumes
- Whole grains like rice, oats, and whole wheat bread
- Fruits and vegetables, which are rich in vitamins and fiber
- Healthy fats such as nuts, seeds, and olive oil

Protein is especially important during weight loss because it helps maintain muscle mass and keeps you feeling full for longer.

Exercise plays a key role in weight loss. A combination of strength training and cardio is recommended. Strength training helps preserve muscle, while cardio helps burn calories.

Examples of cardio include walking, jogging, cycling, and swimming. Beginners can start with 15 to 30 minutes of cardio, 3 to 5 times per week.

Good habits are also important:
- Drink enough water
- Get 7 to 9 hours of sleep
- Reduce processed foods and sugary drinks
- Stay consistent over time

A safe rate of weight loss is about 0.5 to 1 kilogram per week. Rapid weight loss is usually not healthy and can lead to muscle loss.

Weight loss should be approached as a long-term lifestyle change, not a short-term diet.
